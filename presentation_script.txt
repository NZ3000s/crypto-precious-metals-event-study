SLIDE 2 — Why this story matters
################################################################################

Title:
  Why this story matters

Text:
  Gold and silver have long been traded via physical-backed ETFs (GLD, SLV)
  on regulated exchanges.
  In early 2026, Binance listed 24/7 perpetuals on the same underlyings. That
  creates a second venue for the same economic assets.
  We measure whether the incumbent ETF market changed—more volatile, more
  liquid, or more tightly linked to the new venue. That matters for market
  structure, arbitrageurs, and regulators.

Required Tables & Charts:
  None.

Speaker Notes:
  Tell the story: two venues, same asset. Our question is not only “did
  volatility go up?” but “how did the structure of trading change?”

################################################################################
SLIDE 5 — Event and method
################################################################################

Title:
  Event and method

Text:
  Design: Event study with pre/post comparison. No control group → we do not
  use difference-in-differences.
  Tests: (1) Welch t-tests (pre vs post); (2) OLS with outcome = volatility or
  liquidity, regressor = post_event + controls (DXY, VIX, 10Y). HAC
  (Newey–West) SE; winsorization 5%/95%.
  Literature: Augustin et al. (2023), Management Science — impact of Bitcoin
  futures on spot; they use DiD with treatment/control. We have a single
  market before/after, so we use pre/post + OLS with controls.
  See Literature/LITERATURE_METHODS.md.

Required Tables & Charts:
  None (or one bullet “Pre vs Post + OLS with controls”).

Speaker Notes:
  Mention that the Bitcoin futures paper uses DiD; we cannot because we have
  no control group. Our design is standard event study with controls.
